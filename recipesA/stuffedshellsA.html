A Very Cheesy Americanized Stuffed Shells Recipe

This has long been a family favorite. My husband and I make it every time we go up to the family cabin. It is cheesy rich American spin on an Italian dish that goes beyond just the normal spaghetti or lasagna.

Over the years we have adjusted the recipe using a bit more cheese and adding hot italian sausage to make it uniquely our own. We encourage everyone to put their own twist on our favorite meal.

Ingredients

  • Jumbo shells 12 oz
  • Ricotta cheese 38 oz
  • Mozarella cheese 16 oz
  • 2 eggs
  • Parmesan cheese< 8 oz/li>
  • Preferred marinara pasta sauce
  • Dried oregano
  • Dried parsley
  • Optional add-ins mushrooms, Italian sausage
Steps
  1. Start the water to boil to shells.Follow the instructions on the shells box for cooking ot al dente.
  2. While the shells are cooking, mix together Ricotta cheese, 2 eggs, 1/2 the Mozarella, paremsan cheese 1 tsp oregano, and 1 tsp parsley. Mixture can be refrigerated if shells are not done.
  3. Once shells cooked to al dente. Drain and rince shells. Set them aside for a few minutes to cool.
  4. In a casserol dish or disposable alumnium pan, spread a very thin layer of marinara sauce.
  5. Stuff each shell with the Ricotta mixture and place in the casserole dish.
  6. Once all shells are stuffed and in the pan. Pour the remaining sauce over the shells. Sprinkle the last of Mozarella cheese over the top of the stuffed shells.
  7. Bake the stuffed shells for 45 minutes at 350 degrees.
